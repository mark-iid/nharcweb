D-STAR is an open digital voice and data standard for amateur radio, developed by the Japan
Amateur Radio League (JARL). NHARC brought **Pittsburgh's first D-STAR repeater** on the air
in **June 2007**, installed at the WQED TV transmitter building in Oakland.

Today the club operates D-STAR on **W3EXW C (146.82 MHz, 2 m)** and **W3PGH B (444.35 MHz,
70 cm)**. The 146.82 machine hosts the **Western PA D-STAR net every Monday at 9:00 PM**,
linked to reflector **REF063C**.

## Radio programming — W3PGH 70 cm D-STAR

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Receive (RX) | 444.350 MHz |
| Transmit (TX) | 449.350 MHz (+5 MHz) |
| MYCALL | *Your callsign* |
| URCALL | `CQCQCQ` |
| RPT1 | `W3PGH  B` |
| RPT2 | `W3PGH  G` |

> **New to D-STAR?** Your radio must be registered on the D-STAR gateway before you can use
> the routing and reflector features. Ask on a net or email **info@nharc.org** and a club
> member will be glad to help you get set up.

## Supporting the repeaters

The repeaters are maintained by a separately incorporated non-profit repeater association,
supported by member donations. Contributions of **$20 or more** include autopatch privileges
on the 146.88 and 147.09 machines. Tower space is generously provided by WPXI and WQED.
